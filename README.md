# Global Retail Sales & Profitability Analysis

## Project Overview
Analysis of global retail sales data covering 4 years (2011–2014) across 
multiple regions, customer segments, and product categories. The goal was 
to identify sales trends, profit drivers, and high-value customers to 
support business decision-making.

## Tools Used
- **Excel** — Data cleaning, pivot tables, regional profit analysis, 
  product performance analysis, customer segmentation, 
  Pareto (80/20) profitability analysis with charts
- **SQL** — Data exploration, business queries, sales and profit analysis
- **Power BI** — 4-page interactive dashboard covering sales overview, 
  performance analysis, product insights, and geographic distribution

## Key Findings & Business Insights

**Customer Profitability:**
- 52% of customers generate 80% of total profit (Pareto principle)
- Only 24 high-value customers (3%) contribute 151K in profit
- 67 loss-making customers are generating a net loss of -28,747 — 
  the business should review pricing, discounts, or service costs 
  for these accounts and consider discontinuing unprofitable relationships
- Medium-value customers (89% of base) represent the biggest growth 
  opportunity — targeted marketing and upselling strategies focused 
  on this segment could significantly improve overall profitability

**Product Performance:**
- Canon imageCLASS leads both top sales and top profit charts
- A notable gap exists between high-sales and high-profit products, 
  suggesting some top-selling products carry thin margins — 
  pricing strategy review recommended
- Tables sub-category operated at a loss despite decent sales volume

**Regional & Segment Performance:**
- Central region consistently generated the highest profit across all years
- Consumer segment contributed the most to total sales (6.5M)
- Sales and profit grew consistently year over year from 2011 to 2014
